My Work

Full Professor at the University of Belgrade, Faculty of Philosophy

  • Principal investigator in a multi-year project in collaboration with the University of Oxford and the John Templeton Foundation
  • Researcher at the Milton and Ethel Harris Research Initiative for Developmental Psychology, Toronto (2005–2008)
  • Worked with children on the autism spectrum at the Geneva Centre for Autism, Toronto (2003–2005)
  • Academic CV

Philosophy is not a lifeless theory but a bridge between what we are and what we can become.

I am a full professor of philosophy at the University of Belgrade. My academic and professional journey combines a philosophical approach with interdisciplinary research on emotions and human psychology, always with a focus on practical engagement. My education — from a philosophy degree in Belgrade to a master's and doctorate at York University in Canada — has shaped my unique approach, blending ancient wisdom, modern philosophy of mind, and psychology. My doctoral thesis, The Roots of Empathy: Toward an Integrative Model of Empathic Development, reflects my early interest in human psychology and moral development.

Throughout my career, I have explored a wide range of topics — from the history of emotions and philosophy of psychology to the epistemological challenges of faith and transhumanism — always emphasizing the practical application of knowledge. My work on projects such as Science, Faith, and Superstition (supported by the Templeton Foundation) and my time at the Geneva Centre for Autism in Toronto (where I worked with children on the autism spectrum) reveal my desire to bring philosophy closer to everyday life and apply my theoretical knowledge in psychology.

As the author of numerous academic papers and books (such as Happiness: The Philosophy and Psychology of the Good Life), I explore how philosophical traditions — from Stoicism and patristics to modern ethics — can serve as tools for overcoming emotional crises, loss of meaning, or conflicts of values. My work particularly highlights the role of reflection on deeply ingrained beliefs, offering a path toward a more authentic and meaningful existence.

As a philosophical counselor, I use this knowledge to help individuals:

  • Identify and transform emotions,
  • Resolve moral dilemmas through dialogue with different ethical traditions,
  • Find meaning in crisis through reflection.

My mission is not only academic but also therapeutic: to restore philosophy to its original role — the art of living. Through patient dialogue, I help clients move beyond unexamined assumptions about themselves and the world, offering philosophical tools for developing a more authentic identity.
